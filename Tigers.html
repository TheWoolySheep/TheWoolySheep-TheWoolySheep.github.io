Ten Interesting Facts About Tigers

Reference: National Geographic, Britannica, Wikipedia

Fact one: Wild tigers have a life span of up to 15 years.

Fact two: Tigers are good swimmers and actually enjoy water.

Fact three: The scientific name for tigers is panthera tigris.

Fact four: Tigers are beginning to be called the King of the Jungles rather than lions. Afterall, lions don't live in the jungle!

Fact five: No two patterns of stripes on a tiger are the ssame.

Fact six: You can hear a tiger roar from three kilometres away!

Fact seven: There are more tigers in captivity than in the wild.

Fact eight: There is an estimated 4,500 tigers left in the world.

Fact nine: Overall, there are nine known subspecies of tigers. However, three of these have become extinct.

Fact ten: Tigers are hunted for their fur and body parts that can be used in traditional medicines.
